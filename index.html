On the plumage of birds and other matters

Birds are variously either magnificent creatures, tasty snacks or ignoble vermin, depending on whether they're pigeons, chickens or every other kind of bird (in reverse order).

One of the most distinctive features of birds (which helps us determine whether one is a pigeon and should be held in contempt) is their plumage (or feathering for those of us who don't think the word sounds obscene).

Feathering is superior to fur in a number of ways as it permits gliding and soaring, though it is as useless as fur for transporting coconuts to the northern hemisphere.

Pigeons and their detestable character

Although doves receive a decent amount of positive publicity, their foul brethren the pigeons deserve nothing short of contempt. Further, in this authors' opinion, they should be banned.

Many features of this flying freakshow of feathers makes them incompatible with civilised society. Making ridiculous noises, low-flying attacks on pedestrians and perching are just a few of their transgressions.

It is no exaggeration to say that their droppings are inappropriate enough to dissolve the paint on your environmentally unfriendly automobile or soil your only ok-looking suit. As a pigeon-attack survivor, the author can attest to their feverish ferocity for fatalities. This, naturally, precludes any sort of meaningful bond between reasonable people and these feathered fiends.

In conclusion, the airspeed velocity of an unladen swallow may be subject to speculation, but the wordly harm caused by pigeons and their ilk certainly is not.
